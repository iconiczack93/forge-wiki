    Meeting Analysis: HockeyStack 1:1 w/ Hank

    🔑 TL;DR

    The team is restructuring the AI engineer hiring process to move technical screening to the first interview stage while balancing candidate experience concerns and addressing compensation transparency issues that are affecting response rates.

    📋 Action Items

    Hank - Update the interview process Notion doc to move Tech Screen 1 to the first interview stage - Remove compensation range from outreach sequences/messages (keep in job description for legal compliance) - Continue sourcing candidates with 2-7 years of experience (avoiding excessively senior candidates) - Prepare 5-minute opening pitch script for engineers conducting first-round technical screens

    Zack - Work with Hank to improve the sourcing message prompts - Discuss with Arda and team members about updating Technical Round 2 questions for AI-focused roles - Create new technical interview questions focused on AI-specific topics (prompt tuning, memory, context, beyond basic OpenAI/Anthropic API usage) - Train junior engineers (Diaz, Roy, Daniel) on conducting 30-minute first-round interviews with proper opening pitch

    Arda - Collaborate on redesigning Technical Round 2 questions to be more AI-focused

    ✅ Decisions Made

    • Technical Screen 1 will become the first interview stage (moving away from recruiter-first approach)
    • Compensation range will be removed from outreach messages but kept in job descriptions (legal requirement)
    • Tech Screen 1 will be 30 minutes: 10 minutes for company intro/candidate questions, 20 minutes for technical assessment
    • Different interview processes may be applied based on seniority level (more flexibility for senior candidates)
    • Target candidate experience range remains 2-7 years (not pursuing excessively senior engineers)
    • Technical Round 2 needs to be redesigned with AI-specific questions beyond basic API usage

    💬 Talking Points

    • Concerns about candidate experience when technical screening is the first touchpoint (especially for senior engineers who may reject the process)
    • Compensation expectations are higher than anticipated—multiple candidates indicated the posted range ($220K) is low for "good engineers"
    • Importance of maintaining positive employer branding even with rejected/disqualified candidates (currently receiving positive feedback)
    • Title inflation in the market is driving unrealistic compensation expectations for candidates with limited actual AI/ML experience
    • Need to balance speed of hiring with quality of candidate experience and employer brand protection

    📌 Weekly Action List

    • Update Notion doc to reflect new interview process flow with Tech Screen 1 first (Owner: Hank, Priority: High)
    • Remove compensation from outreach sequences while keeping in job descriptions (Owner: Hank, Priority: High)
    • Draft and finalize new AI-focused Technical Round 2 questions (Owner: Zack & Arda, Priority: High)
    • Create 5-minute opening pitch script for first-round technical interviewers (Owner: Hank, Priority: Medium)
    • Train junior engineers on conducting 30-minute first interviews with proper structure (Owner: Zack, Priority: Medium)
    • Refine sourcing message prompts to improve response quality (Owner: Zack & Hank, Priority: Medium)

    ⏰ Deadlines Mentioned

    • April 7th — Team travel to Hawaii for company gathering (Hank arrives at 11:30 AM Hawaii time)
    • Within 2 weeks — Hawaii team gathering event
    • This evening or tomorrow — Zack to create new Technical Round 2 questions (self-imposed deadline)

    ❓ Unresolved Questions

    • Should different interview processes be formalized for different seniority levels, or handled case-by-case?
    • Is the current compensation range competitive enough for the market, or does it need adjustment?
    • Can the job description compensation range legally be removed, or must it stay posted?
    • How realistic is it for candidates to complete both algorithm and SQL questions in 20 minutes during Tech Screen 1?
    • What specific AI topics should be tested in Technical Round 2 beyond API usage?

    📝 Quick Summary

    Zack and Hank discussed restructuring the AI engineer hiring process, deciding to move technical screening to the first interview stage to speed up hiring while maintaining candidate experience through a structured 30-minute format. They addressed concerns about compensation transparency after receiving feedback that their posted range appears low to candidates, deciding to remove it from outreach messages but keep it in job descriptions for legal compliance. The team also identified the need to update Technical Round 2 with more AI-specific questions and will work on improving sourcing messages and training junior engineers to conduct first-round interviews effectively.
